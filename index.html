Cape Cod is one of the most beloved stretches of coastline in America — a curling arm of sand and sea reaching into the Atlantic. It's home to some of the most beautiful lighthouses on the East Coast, each one with its own personality and perch above the waves. This weekend, we follow the Cape from its elbow all the way to the very tip, ending at the edge of the country as the sun sets over Race Point.

🎂   Here's to celebrating a very special birthday on the open road — Atlantic breeze, four incredible lighthouses, and the whole family together.   🎂

The Lighthouse Loop

📍 Google Maps
Stop 1 ·Visit Time ~45 min
Chatham Lighthouse
Chatham · The Elbow of the Cape

Our first beacon marks the dramatic "elbow" where Cape Cod bends north. The lighthouse crowns a bluff above Chatham Harbor, looking out over ever-shifting sandbars that wrecked hundreds of ships over the centuries. The grounds are open and free, the view is stunning, and the charming village of Chatham is steps away — perfect for grabbing coffee and breakfast before heading north.

Free to Visit Scenic Harbor Views Walkable Village

📍 Google Maps
Stop 2 ·Visit Time ~1 hour
Nauset Lighthouse
Eastham · Cape Cod National Seashore

You've seen this lighthouse before — it's the iconic red-and-white tower on every bag of Cape Cod Potato Chips! Volunteer guides bring the history to life inside. The light sits beautifully above Nauset Light Beach, one of the Cape's most photographed shores. Look for seals playing in the surf below. No admission fee; donations welcome.

Free Entry 🌟 Iconic Photo Spot Beach Access Volunteer Tours

📍 Google Maps
Stop 3 ·Visit Time ~1.25 hours
Highland Lighthouse
Truro · Perched 120 Feet Above the Atlantic

The oldest and tallest lighthouse on Cape Cod sits on a dramatic bluff with the Atlantic stretching endlessly in every direction. At $8 per adult, the guided climb to the top is an absolute highlight — the views from the lantern room are extraordinary. It uniquely shares its grounds with a functioning golf course, a purely Cape Cod quirk.

$8 to Climb 🌟 Best Views on the Cape Guided Tour Open 9am–5pm

📍 Google Maps
Stop 4 ·Visit Time ~1.5 hours
Race Point Lighthouse
Provincetown · The Outermost Point of Cape Cod

The grand finale. Race Point sits at the very tip of Cape Cod, where the National Seashore feels wild and utterly untouched. The lighthouse requires a beautiful 1.5-mile walk through rolling sand dunes — a genuine adventure. Gray seals often haul out on the beach nearby, and the golden afternoon light across the dunes is absolutely breathtaking.

1.5-Mile Dune Walk 🦭 Watch for Seals! National Seashore 🌅 Golden Hour Magic

Good to Know Before You Go

⏰
Arrive Early at Highland

Tours can fill up by midday in season. Plan to arrive close to 12:30pm or reserve ahead at recreation.gov. Absolutely worth it.

👟
Wear Good Shoes

Race Point is a 1.5-mile walk through deep sand dunes. Sneakers or trail runners work best. Skip the flip-flops for this one.

🅿️
Parking Notes

Nauset Light Beach parking is $20/day in peak season — covers all Cape Cod National Seashore beaches that day. Worth every penny.

🌅
Time Race Point Right

Arrive between 3–5pm when golden hour light hits the dunes and lighthouse perfectly. The most magical photos of the whole trip.

📸
Photo Tips

Nauset faces east (gorgeous morning light). Highland has open ocean (best midday). Race Point faces northwest — pure magic at golden hour.

Sunday: The Drive Home

Head out after the Comfort Inn's free breakfast and a slow Sunday morning in Hyannis. The drive back to Silver Spring takes about 7.5 hours via I-95 South. Consider a gorgeous pit stop in Newport, Rhode Island — Castle Hill Lighthouse and Beavertail Lighthouse are right off the highway and take just 20 minutes each. A perfect birthday weekend bookend.
